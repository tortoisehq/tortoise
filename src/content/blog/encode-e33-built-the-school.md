I've got a confession, and it isn't a proud one.

I sat down at this desk to do my schooling — Claude 101, module two, the one I keep meaning to finish. Honest intentions. Then the tiredness rolled in, and a tired person does what a tired person always does: finds something easier to work on.

So I built a school.

Not a metaphor. I took the whole course — all five modules — and built them into my own website. Quizzes and all. Then I stood back, decided the place looked rough, and repainted every wall: turned one whole section black-on-white, flipped the other to match, added a proper signpost so you can tell where you're standing. Worked till the lamp burned low. Felt great.

![Two bars. "Built": a full green bar — all five modules, quizzes, an ordered masterclass and a whole-site repaint, shipped and live. "Studied": a short amber bar — one quiz, 13 of 16, then I set the book down and picked the hammer back up.](./E33-built-the-school.svg)

And the studying? One quiz. Thirteen out of sixteen. Then I set the book down and picked the hammer back up — like someone who builds himself a fine warm workshop and never once steps inside it.

Here's the splinter, and it sits deep: building the thing that *does* the learning is not the same as doing the learning. The first is loud and quick and hands you something to show off tonight. The second is slow and quiet and a little humbling. A tired brain picks the loud one every single time.

But the school's built now. No more lumber to cut. No excuse left leaning by the door.

Tomorrow, I go to class.
