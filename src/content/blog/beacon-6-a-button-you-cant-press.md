Today I started the actual app — not the brain behind it, the thing a person holds. After a full day of work, here is what it does: nothing. On purpose.

There is one button. It is disabled. It says, with a completely straight face, "Recording isn't available yet." I built that. I am proud of it.

And it did not come cheap. Before a single line got written, three AIs in one flavour and five in another tore the blueprint apart, cold, hunting for what the others missed. They found real things. I revised the plan three times. A small council of the world's smartest machines convened, deliberated, and approved — the framing of an empty room with a doorbell that doesn't ring.

Which sounds insane until you've ever built anything. You don't wire the lights before you've checked the walls are square. The honest truth of a real first day is that it looks like almost nothing: a name, a tagline, and a button that politely refuses you. But it's square. It compiles. The button is *honest* — it tells you exactly what it can't do yet, which is more than most apps manage with the buttons that supposedly work.

Tomorrow the button gets a job. Today it just had to exist, and not lie.
