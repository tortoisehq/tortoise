![A phone speaks a grievance into a filed, cited report](/images/steward-s17-speak-it-file-it.svg)

Here's the thing nobody warns you about railroading: the job isn't the hard part. You *know* the job. What grinds you down is the **paper** — the grievance you should file but won't, because it's 11pm, you're nineteen hours into a shift, and the form wants ten fields and an article number you'd have to dig out of an agreement thicker than a tie plate.

That's the whole reason Steward exists. You talk. It listens. It turns *"they didn't relieve me when my rest was due"* into a real, cited grievance — with the article **quoted, not guessed.**

This week Steward got a serious upgrade, and the funny part is I barely touched it. I went to lift, and let the thing build itself. Came back to four new pieces:

- **A real home screen.** It used to say "your stuff will show up here," like a fortune cookie. Now it shows your grievances, your recent ones, and a big honest warning when a filing deadline is sneaking up on you.
- **Hear your report out loud.** Because reading a screen after a night shift is its own punishment. Tap the button; Steward reads it back.
- **Review and approve.** See the cited report, check it, approve it. The "send it to the union" part is still behind a locked door — we don't fire off real filings until it's bulletproof — but the door is built now.
- **A spend cap that can't be wiped.** Boring, but it means the thing can't quietly run up a bill if something loops. Adults-in-the-room stuff.

![A rail curving toward a deadline clock, with a filed-in-time checkmark](/images/steward-s17-deadline.svg)

I'll be straight with you: none of this is on your phone yet. It's built, and it's been checked — twice, by a small army of robot reviewers that caught two real bugs I'd otherwise have shipped. Now it's waiting on the last mile.

The dream hasn't changed: **you speak it once, Steward files it right, and the deadline never beats you.** We're closer this week than last — and I was at the gym for most of it.
