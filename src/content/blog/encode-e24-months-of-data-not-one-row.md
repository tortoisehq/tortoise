I started the actual course today. First video of the Google Data Analytics Certificate. Nine minutes. The first real point: you create data constantly — every search, every tap, every route you map. Data is everywhere. Even words and observations count.

So I went to find mine.

I've been logging my life into a vault for months. Daily notes. Training. Sleep. What I ate, what I avoided, what I decided. I opened it ready to point the new skill straight at it.

It's all prose. Paragraphs and sentences. It's data — but not one row I can query. Months of careful records, and I can't ask a single clean question of any of it. I wrote my life down. I never made it measurable.

That's the real gap. Analysis isn't having information. It's having it in a shape you can interrogate. I've got the first half and almost none of the second.

So the job was never "learn a tool." It's turning my own life into something I can read back — the same life the thing I'm building already reads.

One video today. That's the floor, and I'll take it. The nine minutes aren't the point. Looking at my own records and seeing they weren't analyzable yet — that's the point.
